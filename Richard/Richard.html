Biography

Richard Matthew Stallman, often referred to as RMS, is a pivotal figure in computer programming and a prominent advocate for software freedom. Born on March 16, 1953, in New York City, he displayed an early aptitude for mathematics and science. Stallman is best known for his uncompromising dedication to his principles, advocating for software that prioritizes users' rights over corporate control. His work, including founding the GNU Project and the Free Software Foundation, has been instrumental in promoting a more open, ethical, and collaborative approach to software development, leaving a lasting impact on the field.

Early Life and Education

Stallman attended a Columbia University-run program for gifted high school students before enrolling at Harvard University in 1973. He graduated magna cum laude with a bachelor's degree in physics in 1974. During his time at Harvard, Stallman's exceptional programming skills began to shine.

Career Beginnings

After graduating, Stallman became a programmer at the MIT Artificial Intelligence Laboratory in 1971. This experience proved pivotal in shaping his future career and philosophy. He quickly became a regular in the hacker community, where he was known by his initials "RMS".

Key Milestones

  • 1983: Launched the GNU Project to create a free Unix-like operating system
  • 1985: Founded the Free Software Foundation (FSF)
  • 1989: Co-founded the League for Programming Freedom1

Free software philosophy

His free software is defined by four essential freedoms:

  • The freedom to redistribute copies.
  • The freedom to distribute modified versions.
  • The freedom to run the program for any purpose.
  • The freedom to study and modify the source code.

GNU

GNU (is not Unix) it's a free software project that embodies the philosophy of free software.

GNU was initiated by Richard Stallman in 1983 with the goal of creating a completely free Unix-like operating system. The project aimed to provide users with a fully functional system that respects their freedom and fosters collaboration.
