Linus Torvalds: The Linux Legend

Creator of Linux and Pioneer of Open Source Technologies

About Linus Torvalds

Linus Torvalds: A Biography

Linus Benedict Torvalds, born on December 28, 1969, in Helsinki, Finland, is a Finnish-American computer scientist best known for creating the Linux kernel, a core component of the GNU/Linux operating system.

Early Life and Education

From a young age, Linus showed an interest in programming, inspired by his grandfather's Commodore VIC-20. He pursued his studies at the University of Helsinki, where he earned a master's degree in computer science in 1996. His thesis was titled Linux: A Portable Operating System.

Creation of Linux

In 1991, dissatisfied with the MS-DOS operating system on his personal computer, Torvalds decided to develop an alternative inspired by UNIX. Using Maurice J. Bach's book The Design of the Unix Operating System as a guide, he created a kernel that he named "Linux," combining his first name with UNIX. He released this kernel under the GPLv2 license, allowing anyone to modify and improve the source code.

Creation of Git

In 2005, Linus Torvalds created Git, a distributed version control system, to manage the development of the Linux kernel. Git was designed to handle everything from small to very large projects with speed and efficiency. It has since become one of the most widely used version control systems in the world, known for its robustness, flexibility, and distributed nature. Git's creation was driven by the need for a reliable and efficient system to manage the growing complexity of the Linux kernel development.

Professional Career

After university, Torvalds worked at Transmeta in the United States from 1997 to 2003, where he contributed to the design of the Crusoe processor. In 2003, he dedicated himself entirely to Linux kernel development within the Open Source Development Labs (OSDL), which later merged with The Free Standards Group to become the Linux Foundation.

Contributions and Honors

In addition to Linux, Torvalds also created Git, a widely used distributed version control system. He has received numerous accolades for his contributions to computing, including the Millennium Technology Prize in 2012 and the Computer Pioneer Award in 2014. Torvalds is known for his strict oversight of Linux kernel development, earning him the nickname "Benevolent Dictator for Life" (BDFL) of the Linux project. Today, he remains a central figure in open-source development and became a naturalized U.S. citizen in 2010.

Legacy

Linus Torvalds’ work has had a profound impact on software development and open-source communities worldwide. His vision and commitment to collaboration continue to inspire developers and tech enthusiasts around the globe.

Major Achievements

  • Creation of the Linux kernel in 1991 while studying at the University of Helsinki
  • Development of Git version control system in 2005
  • Recipient of the Millennium Technology Prize in 2012
  • Recipient of the Computer Pioneer Award in 2014
  • Induction into the Internet Hall of Fame in 2012
  • Named one of the most influential people in the world by Time magazine in 2004

Fun Fact

He created Linux because he didn’t have money for UNIX

In early 1991, unhappy with MS-DOS and MINIX, Torvalds wanted to buy a UNIX system. Luckily for us, he didn’t have enough money. So he decided to make his own clone of UNIX, from scratch

Linux could have been called Freax

In September 1991, Linus announced Linux (standing for ‘Linus’s MINIX’) and encouraged his colleagues to use its source code for wider distribution. Linus thought that the name Linux was too egotistical. He wanted to change it to Freax (based on free, freak and MINIX), but his friend Lemmarke had already created a directory called Linux on his FTP server. And thus the name Linux continued.

Steve Jobs wanted him to work on Apple’s macOS

In 2000, Apple’s founder Steve Jobs invited him to work on Apple’s macOS. Linus refused the lucrative offer and continued to work on the Linux kernel.

Linus has an asteroid named after him

He has numerous awards to his name, including an asteroid named 9793 Torvalds.
